Donor Information Center

Blood donation guidelines, eligibility criteria, and important facts
Eligibility Criteria

Healthy adults meeting age, weight, hemoglobin, and health screening requirements.

Donation Interval

Whole blood donors can typically donate again after 56 days (8 weeks).

Common Myths

Regular donation is safe for healthy individuals. Body replenishes blood naturally.

Research Updates

Studies show potential health benefits from regular donation for healthy donors.

Screening Guidelines
  • Age typically 18-65 years
  • Minimum hemoglobin levels required
  • No active infections or fever
  • Recent tattoos/piercings may defer
  • Pregnant/breastfeeding donors deferred
Donation Impact
  • One donation saves up to 3 lives
  • Supports trauma, surgery, cancer care
  • Essential for emergency services
  • Maintains national blood supply
  • WHO priority health intervention
