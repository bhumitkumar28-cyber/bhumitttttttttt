One Donation, Many Lives

A single blood donation can help save up to 3 lives, which is why one small act can create a huge impact for patients in surgery, trauma, and chronic illness care. [web:18][web:15]

Fast Donation Process

The actual whole-blood donation usually takes less than 10 to 12 minutes, while the full process with checks and paperwork is still typically short. [web:15][web:13]

Recovery Myth Busted

You do not lose your whole blood supply when donating; the body replaces fluid quickly, and red blood cells rebuild over the following weeks. [web:13][web:16]

Eligibility
Healthy adults can donate
Eligibility depends on age, weight, hemoglobin, blood pressure, and medical history. [web:18][web:19]
Repeat Donation
Whole blood every 56 days
A healthy donor may donate whole blood about every 56 days, depending on local rules. [web:16][web:15]
New Research
Possible donor benefits
Recent studies suggest regular donation may influence iron levels, blood pressure, insulin sensitivity, and blood-cell genetics, though research is still evolving. [web:14][web:20]
Safety First
Safe blood is essential
WHO says blood transfusion saves lives and every country should maintain safe and adequate blood supply systems. [web:21]
Common Myth
Donation weakens you forever
This is false; healthy people recover from donation, and donors are screened to make sure donation is appropriate. [web:13][web:19]
What Patients Need
RBCs, platelets, plasma
Hospitals rely on red cells, platelets, and plasma for emergencies, surgery, cancer care, and chronic disease treatment. [web:12][web:18]

Donation awareness message

Blood donation is not only a life-saving service for patients, it is also a highly organized, screened, and carefully timed process designed to protect both donor and recipient.
